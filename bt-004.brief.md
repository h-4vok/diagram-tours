# BT-004 Design Brief

This file is the living design brief for BT-004 and will be removed at the end of the work.

## Summary

BT-004 is a full visual redesign of `diagram-tours`. The goal is to transform the app from a functional engineering tool into a polished, premium diagram-reading experience while keeping the same core workflow:

1. open a local repo, folder, diagram file, or tour
2. inspect the diagram in a browser
3. move through the walkthrough step by step
4. orient with zoom, minimap, and issues

The redesign should make the UI feel intentional, cohesive, and technically credible. The current product already works well enough functionally; BT-004 is about elevating the visual language, hierarchy, spacing, panel sizing, and diagram readability so the whole surface feels designed rather than assembled.

## What We Want

- A premium graphite/slate dark mode, not a generic black UI
- A clear, dense, highly readable desktop layout
- Stronger hierarchy between the canvas, the step navigator, the minimap, and the diagnostics
- Better label, connector, and focus-state readability on Mermaid diagrams
- A more deliberate "tool for experts" feel, similar in seriousness to Linear, Vercel, or Raycast, but still unique to diagram exploration

## Design Pillars

1. Visual systems first
   - tokens, typography, surfaces, borders, shadows, radii, theme contrast

2. Screen architecture
   - shell, canvas, header, right-side control cluster, bottom teleprompter, modal search, issues surface

3. Diagram legibility
   - active nodes, inactive dimming, connector emphasis, panning behavior, Mermaid theming

4. Readability and flow
   - step text as the "subtitled" layer of the experience
   - search and issues as fast utility surfaces, not clutter

## Current Direction

- The app should feel immersive, not split into disconnected floating widgets.
- Controls should read as a coherent system, not as separate boxes.
- The diagram remains the hero, but the surrounding UI should frame it with stronger visual order.
- All the panel widths, alignment, and spacing should feel deliberate.
- The experience should remain functional for dense technical diagrams and large stress-test diagrams.

## Scope Areas

1. Design Tokens and Theming
   - color palette
   - typography
   - elevation and blur
   - radii and semantic states

2. Global Layout
   - full-screen shell
   - header
   - control cluster placement
   - stacking and spacing

3. Step Navigator
   - the bottom teleprompter-style panel
   - readable step content
   - progress and keyboard affordances

4. Command Palette
   - keyboard-first tour search
   - categorized results
   - focused selection states

5. Diagram Focus and Camera Behavior
   - focus states
   - dimming
   - strong connector treatment
   - smooth auto-panning

6. Issues Surface
   - diagnostic hierarchy
   - readable issue cards
   - empty state

## Non-Negotiables

- Do not lose the diagram-first workflow.
- Do not make the interface feel more generic in the process of simplifying it.
- Do not reduce clarity of zoom, minimap, step navigation, or issues.
- Preserve the app's technical, precise personality.
